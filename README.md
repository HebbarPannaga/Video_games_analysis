# Video Game Market Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores video game industry trends using Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn. The analysis combines exploratory and diagnostic analytics to uncover key factors associated with commercial success, player engagement, publisher performance, and genre popularity.

The objective is to move beyond simple descriptive reporting and identify patterns that may explain why certain games, genres, and publishers outperform others.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains information on video game titles, including:

- Sales performance
- Genre and platform information
- Publisher details
- User and critic ratings
- Review counts
- Multiplayer support
- DLC availability
- Microtransactions
- Game of the Year (GOTY) nominations and wins
- Sequel information

## Key Findings

- Sports, Action, and Shooter genres generate the largest share of industry revenue.
- Console and Mobile platforms dominate overall sales.
- Publisher revenue is strongly associated with portfolio size.
- Sequels consistently outperform non-sequel titles in average sales.
- Award-winning and nominated games achieve higher commercial success.
- Higher-rated games generally perform better commercially.
- Several highly-rated games remain commercially underrecognized, representing potential growth opportunities.
- Multiplayer support contributes positively to commercial performance in many cases.

---

## Business Insights

- Franchise-based game development appears to reduce commercial risk and increase revenue potential.
- Industry recognition through awards can significantly improve visibility and sales performance.
- Portfolio scale is an important contributor to publisher revenue.
- High-quality games do not always achieve strong commercial success, highlighting the importance of marketing and distribution strategies.
- Player satisfaction and commercial performance are related but not always perfectly aligned.
